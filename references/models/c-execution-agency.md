# Category C — Execution & Agency

*How to get things done and where leverage lives.*

Owned by the **Execution & Agency panel agent** in panel mode. 9 models. Each entry: what it is, what blindspot it catches, the sparring prompt that weaponises it. In solo mode, loaded directly as part of the deep bench.

---

**High Agency**
Most constraints you accept as real aren't. The question isn't "can we?" — it's "how do we?" High agency means treating obstacles as information, not verdicts.
*Catches:* Accepting false limits as fixed; framing problems as blocked when the block is negotiable; waiting for permission that was never required.
*Sparring prompt:* "Is that a real constraint, or is it a story you're telling yourself?"

**Prerequisites**
Before a plan works, certain conditions must be true. Most plans skip the foundational work and assume readiness that doesn't exist.
*Catches:* Launching before the scaffolding is in place; assuming team skill or market readiness without verifying; building floor 3 before floor 1 is solid.
*Sparring prompt:* "What has to be true before this plan can work? Have you verified those things?"

**Activation Energy**
Every change requires more energy to initiate than to sustain. The catalyst to start is a different problem from the engine that runs the thing.
*Catches:* Investing in steady-state without solving the ignition problem; assuming a new behavior will just happen; underestimating the jolt needed to get something off the ground.
*Sparring prompt:* "What's the ignition event? Who provides the energy to start — not just to sustain?"

**Inertia**
Objects in motion stay in motion; objects at rest stay at rest. Organizations, habits, and markets follow the same law.
*Catches:* Underestimating resistance when changing a running system; underestimating momentum when trying to stop something already moving; assuming change happens at the speed of the decision.
*Sparring prompt:* "What's already in motion that will resist this plan? What's stuck at rest that you're counting on moving?"

**Velocity**
Speed in a specific direction. Not just how fast, but where you're pointed. High speed in the wrong direction is worse than slow speed in the right one.
*Catches:* Moving fast toward the wrong goal; confusing output volume with progress; high throughput on the wrong thing.
*Sparring prompt:* "Are you moving fast in the right direction, or just moving fast?"

**Friction and Viscosity**
The hidden resistance in any system — micro-costs, delays, and effort that accumulate and slow adoption, execution, or change. Friction doesn't announce itself; it compounds quietly.
*Catches:* Assuming smooth adoption of new processes or products; ignoring small resistances that kill good ideas in implementation; underestimating how friction accumulates.
*Sparring prompt:* "Where is friction accumulating in this system that you haven't accounted for?"

**Leverage**
A small input, applied at the right point, produces a disproportionately large output. The question isn't how hard you push — it's where you put the fulcrum.
*Catches:* Working hard on low-leverage activities when force multipliers are available; confusing effort with impact; not asking what would give 10x the output for the same input.
*Sparring prompt:* "Where's the lever here? What single change would give you disproportionate return?"

**Asymmetry**
The best bets have capped downside and uncapped upside. Most of what you do should be asymmetric in your favor.
*Catches:* Taking symmetric or negative-EV risks; treating a small chance of ruin the same as a small chance of gain; not asking "what's the worst case and can I survive it?"
*Sparring prompt:* "What's the downside if this is wrong? Is it capped? Can you live with it?"

**Luck Surface Area**
Luck = doing × telling. The more you do and share openly, the more luck finds you. It's not random — it's a function of exposure.
*Catches:* Working in isolation; misattributing luck to pure randomness when it can be cultivated; hoarding progress updates; confusing being busy with being visible.
*Sparring prompt:* "Are you doing the things that surface luck, or are you waiting for it to find you?"
