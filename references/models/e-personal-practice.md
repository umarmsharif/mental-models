# Category E — Personal Practice

*Behavioral and mental discipline models.*

Owned by the **Personal Practice panel agent** in panel mode. 9 diagnostic models plus practice anchors. Each entry: what it is, what blindspot it catches, the sparring prompt that weaponises it. In solo mode, loaded directly as part of the deep bench.

---

**Acceptance**
Fighting reality consumes energy. Acknowledging what is true — even when unwelcome — is the foundation of clear action. Acceptance is not approval.
*Catches:* Emotional drag from resisting facts; wasting cycles on what should have been instead of what is; making decisions from a distorted picture because the real one is uncomfortable.
*Sparring prompt:* "Are you making decisions from the world as it is, or the world as you wish it were?"

**Visualization / Simulation**
Run the plan in your head until failure modes appear. An untested mental model is a plan you haven't stress-tested.
*Catches:* Vague plans that haven't been mentally rehearsed for failure; skipping the rehearsal that would surface the first point of breakdown.
*Sparring prompt:* "Walk me through exactly how this works, step by step, on the worst reasonable day."

**5 Whys**
Ask why five times. Each answer reveals a deeper cause. The fifth answer is usually close to the root.
*Catches:* Stopping at surface reasons; fixing the presenting problem while the systemic cause keeps producing new ones.
*Sparring prompt:* "That's one why. Ask why again."

**10/10/10**
How will I feel about this decision in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years? Time-shifting reveals which concerns are real and which are noise.
*Catches:* Over-weighting short-term discomfort; under-weighting long-term consequences; treating urgent and important as synonymous.
*Sparring prompt:* "How does this look in 10 years? Does that change anything you'd do today?"

**Mental Momentum**
Getting started is disproportionately hard. Once moving, continuing is much easier. The first step reduces resistance to the second.
*Catches:* Waiting for perfect conditions before starting; underestimating how one small step changes the psychological difficulty of the next.
*Sparring prompt:* "What's the smallest first step that creates momentum? Have you taken it?"

**Intuitive Thinking**
Your intuition encodes pattern-matched expertise. It's fast inference from compressed experience. Don't override it without a reason better than "the data says otherwise."
*Catches:* Rationalizing away a clear gut signal; ignoring proprioceptive knowledge because it can't be verbalized; trusting the deck over the field.
*Sparring prompt:* "What does your gut say? Has it had a fair hearing before you built the case against it?"

**Not Judging**
Suspend evaluation until you have sufficient signal. Premature judgment closes off information and locks in conclusions before they're earned.
*Catches:* Closing options too early; letting assessment mode kill exploration mode; making irreversible calls with reversible-level evidence.
*Sparring prompt:* "Have you given this enough time and surface area before evaluating it?"

**Opportunity Magnet**
You attract the opportunities you're positioned for. Positioning precedes luck. What finds you is a function of what you are, not just what you want.
*Catches:* Wondering why the right opportunities don't appear; failing to see that current positioning predetermines what arrives; expecting different results from the same configuration.
*Sparring prompt:* "What does your current positioning attract? Is that what you want to be attracting?"

**Honesty**
Your thinking is only as good as your willingness to name what you actually believe, not what is safe to say or polite to claim.
*Catches:* Diplomatic hedging that prevents real diagnosis; softening assessments until they lose signal; telling yourself a comfortable story instead of the true one.
*Sparring prompt:* "What do you actually believe here, versus what you've been willing to say out loud?"

**Thanking · Not Planning · Mental Discipline**
Retained as personal practice anchors. Applied to attitude, presence, and orientation — not used as diagnostic instruments in sparring. The panel agent does not score these.
